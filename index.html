All About Apple.

Steve Jobs & The Apple Story.

Steve Jobs, co-founder of Apple, believed that technology
should be intuitive, beautifully designed, and seamlessly
integrated into everyday life. What started as a small garage
project in 1976 grew into a global technology leader under
his vision.

Apple became more than a company. It established itself as a
main driver of innovation, influencing how the world
communicates, listens to music, makes payments, and interacts
with digital products. Its design philosophy and ecosystem
approach set new standards and created a lasting impact on
modern technology and culture.

Major Apple Innovations.

1976

Apple I released — the first computer built by Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak in a garage.

1984

Launch of Macintosh — introducing the graphical user interface to personal computing.

1997

Steve Jobs returns — strategic reset and the beginning of Apple's modern era.

2001

iPod revolutionizes music consumption worldwide.

2007

iPhone changes how humans interact with technology forever.

2010

iPad launches — creating a new device category between laptops and phones.

2015

Apple Watch introduces wearable tech to the mainstream market.

2020

Apple Silicon (M1) transforms computing efficiency and performance.

A Decade of Growth: Apple’s Stock Price Evolution.

How innovation and market sentiment shaped Apple’s stock from 2016–2025

Momentum With Milestones.

Apple’s share price rose sharply following major events like the iPhone X launch (2017) and the stock split (Aug 2020).

Resilience During Crises.

Despite the COVID-19 shock (Mar 2020), Apple recovered quickly, reflecting strong investor confidence in its innovation roadmap.

Silicon Era Transformation.

The shift to Apple Silicon (M1, 2020) improved performance and efficiency, fueling sustained stock momentum.

Apple’s Growth Blueprint.

A visual breakdown of where innovation turns into revenue

  • iPhone.

    Still the core of Apple’s business, accounting for just over 50% of 2024 revenue, thanks to strong Pro model demand.

  • Services.

    Apple’s fastest-growing segment, driven by subscriptions and App Store revenue, now contributes over 20% of total sales.

  • Wearables.

    Apple Watch and AirPods continue expanding, forming an ecosystem that strengthens customer retention.

  • Mac.

    Powered by Apple Silicon, Mac sales stabilized at a higher baseline, cementing Apple’s independence from Intel chips.

  • iPad.

    Post pandemic normalization brought steady demand as the iPad remained dominant in the tablet category.

iPhone’s Global Footprint, 2024.

How regional demand divides Apple’s flagship market

Biggest Gap.

Switzerland takes about 3 days while Brazil takes more than 2 months to afford an iPhone, revealing a 20-times difference that reflects global income inequality.

Regional Comparison.

Japan and South Korea need less than 10 days, whereas China and India require between 20 and 45 days, showing how wage levels and economic development influence affordability across Asia.

Malaysia’s Position.

Malaysia averages around 21 days, close to the global mean, suggesting a middle-income economy where iPhones are affordable but still considered premium products.
